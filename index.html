About Me

Welcome to my academic website. I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from XYZ University in 20XX.

My research focuses on optimization theory, game theory, and their applications to control systems and machine learning. I am particularly interested in developing algorithms for networked systems with multiple decision makers.

Prior to joining UH Manoa, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at ABC University, where I collaborated on projects related to distributed optimization in smart grid systems.

Research Interests

  • Game Theory and Multi-agent Systems
  • Convex and Non-convex Optimization
  • Control Theory
  • Machine Learning
  • Smart Grid and Energy Systems

My research lab is actively seeking motivated graduate students. If you are interested in working with me, please send me an email with your CV and research interests.

Selected Publications

A Game-Theoretic Approach to Distributed Optimization in Multi-agent Systems

Arslan, G., Smith, J., & Johnson, K.
IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 2022

This paper presents a novel approach to solving distributed optimization problems in systems with multiple decision-makers using game-theoretic concepts.

Learning Nash Equilibria in Non-cooperative Games

Arslan, G., & Brown, N.
Conference on Learning Theory (COLT), 2021

We develop an algorithm for learning Nash equilibria in non-cooperative games with provable convergence guarantees.

Distributed Resource Allocation in Smart Grid Systems

Lee, R., Arslan, G., & Patel, S.
IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, 2020

This work proposes a distributed algorithm for optimal resource allocation in smart grid systems with renewable energy sources.

Robust Control Design for Uncertain Systems with Applications to Renewable Energy Integration

Arslan, G., & Chen, H.
American Control Conference, 2019

A framework for designing robust controllers for systems with uncertainty, with applications to renewable energy integration in power grids.

Teaching

EE 416: Control Systems

This course covers the fundamentals of linear control systems, including system modeling, stability analysis, and controller design.

EE 621: Advanced Optimization Theory

A graduate-level course on optimization theory, including convex optimization, duality theory, and algorithms for constrained optimization.

EE 505: Game Theory for Engineers

Introduction to game theory concepts with applications to engineering problems, including Nash equilibria, mechanism design, and learning in games.
